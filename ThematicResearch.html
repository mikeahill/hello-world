Kervaire Invariant One

Hopkins, Ravenel, and I showed that there are manifolds of Kervaire invariant in at most six dimensions. Work of Browder turned this question in geometric topology into one about the survival of certain, well-studied elements in the Adams spectral sequence. Our proof built on work of Ravenel from the 1970s, as recast by Hopkins and Miller in the 1990s, translating the problem to one in equivariant stable homotopy. This necessitated building a suite a tools inspired by motivic homotopy and representation theory.

Chromatic Homotopy Computations

A longstanding feature of my research is the study of explicit computations in chromatic homotopy theory, working to understand large-scale, structural phenomena by building up families of computations. My main focus is on the Hopkins--Miller higher real $K$-theory spectra. For any perfect field $k$ of characteristic $p$ and formal group law $\Gamma$ over $k$ of finite height $n$, the Goerss--Hopkins--Miller theorem produces a commutative ring spectrum lift of the Lubin--Tate universal deformation. For any finite subgroup $G$ of the automorphisms of $\Gamma$, we then have an associated Hopkins--Miller spectrum $EO_n(G)$. These interpolate between the computationally simple Lubin--Tate theories (also called Morava $E$-theory) and the mysterious but much-desired $K(n)$-local sphere.

Equivariant computations in chromatic homotopy

The new tools like the norm and slice filtration introduced in the solution to the Kervaire Invariant One problem gave new ways to structure computations with Hopkins-Miller spectra and related chromatic computations. Using the Hahn-Shi Real orientation of any of the Lubin-Tate $E$-theories at the prime $2$, we can witness the norm to $G$ of Real bordism as a kind of universal, global version of the theories $EO_n(G)$. This gives powerful, transchromatic behavior and new ways to understand classical questions like orientability for these theories.

Equivariant Homotopy

Slice Filtration

A key tool in the solution to the Kervaire Invariant One problem was a generalization of Dugger's $C_2$-equivariant slice filtration to arbitrary finite groups. This new equivariant filtration has some unexpected features and better multiplicative properties than classically studied filtrations.

Equivariant operads & Algebras

One of the new guiding maxims in equivariant stable homotopy is that we should, in any construction parameterized by some set, also consider the possibility that the group acts on the parameterizing set. In computations, this gives rise to transfers and multiplicative norm maps. Categorifying, this gives us functors like induction and the norm functor.

$G$-symmetric monoidal categories

Hopkins and I developed the notion of a $G$-symmetric monoidal category, axiomatizing what is seen with the connections between the representations of a group and its subgroups, with the categories of genuine $G$-spectra and those of $H$-spectra for subgroups $H$, and for more general contexts. Part of this was developed to understand some of the curious features that connect Bousfield localization and equivariant operadic algebras.

  • Equivariant symmetric monoidal structures (Joint with Hopkins)
  • Equivariant Multiplicative Closure (Joint with Hopkins)
  • Equivariant chromatic localizations and commutativity

$N_\infty$-operads

In trying to understand how the transfer arises in equivariant infinite loop spaces and how the norms arise in various equivariant operadic algebras in genuine $G$-spectra, Blumberg and I discovered a new class of equivariant operads, the $N_\infty$-operads. These provide a robust generalization of the classical $E_\infty$-operads, parameterizing a coherently associative and commutative multiplication, but they also structure ``twisted'' multiplications which arise as norm or transfer maps.

  • Operadic multiplications in equivariant spectra, norms, and transfers (Joint with Blumberg)
  • G-symmetric monoidal categories of modules over equivariant commutative ring spectra (Joint with Blumberg)
  • Equivariant stable categories for incomplete systems of transfers (Joint with Blumberg)
  • On the algebras over equivariant little disks

Incomplete Tambara Functors

The zeroth homotopy Mackey functor of an equivariant commutative ring spectrum has a significant amount of extra structure: in addition to a commutative, associative multiplication (making it a `"commutative Green functor"), it has multiplicative norm maps. This makes it into a TNR functor, as studied by Tambara. Blumberg and I studied the algebraic shadow of $N_{\infty}$ ring spectra, describing what happens more generally and unpacking the categorical consequences.

  • Incomplete Tambara Functors (Joint with Blumberg)
  • The right adjoint to the equivariant operadic forgetful functor on incomplete Tambara functors (Joint with Blumberg)
  • On the Andre-Quillen homology of Tambara functors
  • Bi-incomplete Tambara functors (With Blumberg)

K-Theory and THH

The "trace methods" approach to algebraic $K$-theory uses the natural circle action on topological Hochschild homology to build a quite good approximation to algebraic $K$-groups.

Topological Hochschild homology and the norm functor

The careful treatment of equivariant multiplicative constructions like the norm functor allowed for a variety of new constructions and equivariant computations related to topological Hochschild homology. In particular, we can recast $THH$ as an $S^1$-equivariant version of the norm, which allows us to describe other, relative versions. This also gives new computational approaches and a refinement of the classical Hochschild homology that connects with the equivariance more directly.

  • The Witt vectors for Green functors (Joint with Blumberg, Gerhardt, and Lawson)
  • Topological cyclic homology via the norm (Joint with Angeltveit, Blumberg, Gerhardt, Lawson, and Mandell)
  • Interpreting the Bokstedt smash product as the norm (Joint with Angeltveit, Blumberg, Gerhardt, and Lawson)
